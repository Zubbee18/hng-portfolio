Backend Engineers — Stage 8b
You've spent weeks building real systems under real pressure. This stage is different. No new feature to ship. Just you, your work, and a page that shows what kind of engineer you are.
A reviewer should open your portfolio, spend three to five minutes on it, and walk away knowing exactly what you can build. That's the goal.

What to Build
A portfolio page focused on your backend work during HNG. Stack is your call: plain HTML/CSS, React, Next.js, Django templates, or any simple static site tool. Clean and readable beats flashy.

What Goes In It

1. Profile Your name, role/title, short bio, location or timezone, and contact details.

2. HNG Projects Every significant project or task you completed during the internship. For each one:

Project name
Short description
Tech stack
What you personally built
Any useful proof: screenshot, demo note, or deployment link if available

3. Backend Skills The skills you used: API design, authentication, databases, background jobs, testing, logging, deployment, queues, caching, documentation. Each one should point back to a project. If it can't, leave it out.

4. Featured Project Deep Dive

Pick one project and go deeper:

The problem it solved
The architecture or request flow
Key endpoints or modules
One technical challenge and how you solved it

5. Learning Reflection A short section on what you learned during HNG and how you improved as a backend developer. Be specific and honest.

6. Contact Make it easy for someone to reach you.

What We're Looking For

Clear, honest presentation of your backend work
Straight-to-the-point writing. No filler, no exaggerated claims, no copied buzzwords
Projects include enough technical detail to prove you understand what you built
Clean structure and readable layout
Contact information is easy to find
A portfolio that can be reviewed in 3-5 minutes

Criteria

Profile section is clear and professional
HNG projects are listed with description, stack, and personal contribution
Backend skills are specific and backed by project evidence
Featured project deep dive explains the problem, architecture, endpoints/modules, and one challenge solved
Learning reflection is honest, specific, and concise
Layout is clean, readable, and easy to navigate
Submission note includes stack used and portfolio intent
Contact details and any included proof/demo links are correct

What to Submit

The portfolio page or live URL
A short note covering:
The stack you used
What you wanted the portfolio to communicate
